metadata guide

Metadata Basics, which I created during my time as the Cataloging & Metadata Graduate Research Assistant at the University of Texas Libraries, is an online metadata guide covering key concepts and community needs for a variety of widely applied standards.

database management

Working in a team, I developed a database from the ground up using MySQL and MariaDB to create the table structures, and HTML/CSS and PHP to create a dynamic website, incorporating Google Fusion Tables. The database aggregated information about historical Texas criminals.


Our team also developed training materials for the INNER JOIN in SQL.


archival enterprise

Along with two other team members, I processed a collection of more than 2,500 20th century photographs and ephemera related to professional strongman Ottley R. Coulter for the H.J. Lutcher Stark Center, University of Texas at Austin. I also researched for and created an EAD-encoded, DACS compliant finding aid available through Texas Archival Resources Online (TARO) and the Stark Center online archive.
